Richard M. Schulze

Founder of Best Buy

Richard Michael Schulze, The Founder of Best Buy.

Here's a Timeline of Mr. Schulze's Life:

  • 1941 - Born in Saint Paul, Minnesota.
  • 1966 - Mortgaging his home, he founded an audio equipment store named Sound of Music in Saint Paul. Soon after renamed the store to "Best Buy."
  • 1989 - Eliminated the commission model (customers disliked aggressive sales) and expanded Best Buy's offerings to include computer software, music, movies, and computer hardware.
  • 1994 - Increased the average store size to between 45,000 and 58,000 square feet.
  • 2000 - Donated $50 million to the University of St. Thomas, at the time the largest donation to a Minnesota college or university.
  • 2012 - He announced that he was stepping down as Best Buy chairman.
  • 2013 - Rejoined the company that he founded with a new title: chairman emeritus.
  • 2016 - Was ranked #722 on the Forbes 2016 list of the world's billionaires with a net worth of US$2.4 billion.
